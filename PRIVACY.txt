TREVO - PRIVACY POLICY
Last Updated: March 23, 2026

1. OVERVIEW
trevo is a desktop voice-to-text application that runs locally on your Windows
computer. This privacy policy explains what data is collected, how it is used,
and how it is stored.

2. DATA WE COLLECT

2.1 Audio Data
  - trevo captures audio from your microphone ONLY when you explicitly activate
    recording via a keyboard shortcut, voice wake phrase, or clap detection.
  - Audio is temporarily held in memory and sent to your configured speech-to-
    text provider for transcription.
  - Audio is NOT stored on disk unless you enable the "save_audio" option.

2.2 Transcripts
  - Transcribed text is stored locally in a SQLite database on your machine.
  - Transcripts include: raw text, polished text, timestamp, language, and the
    name of the active application window at the time of recording.

2.3 Knowledge Vault
  - Notes, daily logs, and knowledge files are stored as .md files in a local
    folder you choose during setup (default: ~/trevo-vault/).
  - These files never leave your machine.

2.4 Voice Embeddings
  - If you enroll for speaker recognition, a mathematical embedding of your
    voice (256-dimensional vector) is saved locally as a .npy file.
  - This is NOT a recording of your voice — it cannot be used to reconstruct
    your speech.

2.5 Configuration
  - API keys and preferences are stored in a local config.toml file.
  - This file is in your app installation directory and is NOT transmitted
    anywhere.

3. THIRD-PARTY DATA SHARING
When you use cloud-based services, the following data is sent to your chosen
providers:

  | Provider       | Data Sent                    | Purpose            |
  |----------------|------------------------------|--------------------|
  | Groq           | Audio chunks or text         | STT / LLM          |
  | Google Cloud   | Audio chunks or text         | STT / TTS          |
  | OpenAI         | Audio chunks or text         | STT / LLM          |
  | Anthropic      | Text                         | LLM                |
  | Ollama (local) | Text (stays on your machine) | LLM                |

Each provider has its own privacy policy. We encourage you to review them.

If you use ONLY local providers (Whisper Local + Ollama), NO data leaves your
machine.

4. DATA WE DO NOT COLLECT
  - We do not collect analytics or telemetry.
  - We do not track your usage.
  - We do not collect personal information.
  - We do not have accounts or user registration.
  - We do not operate any servers that receive your data.

5. DATA STORAGE AND SECURITY
  - All data is stored locally on your machine.
  - API keys are stored in plaintext in config.toml. We recommend setting
    appropriate file permissions.
  - Future versions may support Windows Credential Store for secure key
    storage.

6. DATA DELETION
  - Uninstalling trevo removes the application files.
  - Your Memory Vault, transcripts database, and config.toml remain on your
    machine until you manually delete them.
  - To fully remove all data:
    1. Uninstall trevo
    2. Delete your Memory Vault folder (default: ~/trevo-vault/)
    3. Delete %LOCALAPPDATA%\trevo\

7. CHILDREN'S PRIVACY
trevo is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect data
from children.

8. CHANGES TO THIS POLICY
This policy may be updated in future releases. Changes will be noted in the
release changelog.
